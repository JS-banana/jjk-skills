# Clearance

Clearance is evidence-scoped preflight. It reduces avoidable surprises; it does
not prove ownership, availability, or legal safety.

## Match Depth to Stakes

- **Internal or temporary name**: optional exact search.
- **Public project**: web search plus the relevant hosting or package registry.
- **Website or community**: add domain and relevant handle checks.
- **Commercial product or brand**: add same-category and phonetic-neighbor
  searches, target-market language checks, and preliminary trademark research.
- **Company or high-investment launch**: prepare evidence for qualified counsel
  in the intended jurisdictions and classes.

## Search Order

1. Exact quoted name and common spacing or hyphen variants.
2. Likely pronunciations, misspellings, and close visual forms.
3. Same category, adjacent category, and target market.
4. Subject-specific namespaces: GitHub, package registries, app stores, domains,
   social platforms, publication catalogs, or event listings.
5. Relevant trademark databases and classes for commercial use.

Use live sources. Record the query surface, date, and what was actually found.

## Status Language

- **No obvious blocker**: scoped searches found no material conflict.
- **Crowded**: multiple close uses weaken searchability or distinction.
- **Blocker**: a strong same-name or confusingly similar use exists in scope.
- **Unknown**: a required source, language, registry, class, or jurisdiction was
  not checked.

Do not write `available`, `trademark-safe`, `globally clear`, or `unique` unless
the statement's exact scope and evidence support it. Domain registration,
company registration, package availability, and trademark rights are separate
questions.

## Legal Boundary

Preliminary database search is not a legal opinion. Similarity, goods and
services, jurisdiction, existing use, and filing status all matter. For a
commercial commitment, hand the Shortlist and search evidence to a qualified
trademark professional.
