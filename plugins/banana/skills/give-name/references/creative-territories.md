# Creative Territories

Use this reference to create semantic distance before creating names. A
Territory is a world of meaning, not a language or a list of synonyms.

## Territory Card

For each Territory, state:

- **Promise**: what the name should make the audience expect
- **Image world**: objects, actions, places, relationships, or cultural ideas
- **Construction moves**: how names may be formed
- **Distance**: how directly it describes the Naming Subject
- **Failure mode**: the cliché or mismatch to avoid

## Sources of Meaning

Choose only those earned by the Brief:

- Role or relationship: ally, guide, witness, challenger, host
- User outcome or transformation: clarity, momentum, relief, mastery
- Action: weave, gather, forge, reveal, align
- Object or instrument: compass, loom, lens, bridge, vessel
- Place or spatial idea: harbor, frontier, orbit, commons
- Nature or physical behavior: current, root, flock, ember, tide
- Human quality: candor, patience, nerve, care
- System principle: feedback, rhythm, convergence, balance
- Culture, literature, history, or philosophy: use verified concepts, not
  decorative exoticism
- Counter-category: a world deliberately unlike the category's dominant code

Do not default to relationship metaphors for every AI tool or to classical
languages for every technical product.

## Construction Moves

Vary form as well as meaning:

- Existing word used directly
- Suggestive or metaphorical word
- Metonym: a related object or detail stands for the whole
- Verb or action form
- Compound of familiar elements
- Clipping or reshaping of an attested word
- Coined blend with traceable components
- Phonetic invention designed around an intended feeling
- Borrowing or culturally grounded form with verified spelling and meaning
- Phono-semantic recreation across languages

Initialisms, deliberate misspellings, and forced suffixes are last resorts. Use
them only when the Brief gives them a job.

## Anti-Homogeneity Check

Before evaluation, inspect the Longlist:

- Are most names built from the same root or suffix?
- Could the names belong to any generic AI, SaaS, or lifestyle product?
- Do all Territories express the same promise in different words?
- Are foreign roots doing real semantic work or only signaling prestige?
- Does the set include different grammatical shapes and sound profiles?

If the answers reveal clustering, reopen the weakest Territory instead of
adding spelling variants.

## Provenance Labels

- **Attested word**: the candidate is a real word in the stated language.
- **Attested root**: the root is verified; the candidate is a new derivation.
- **Coined blend**: the components and transformation are explicit.
- **Inspired form**: the candidate borrows a sound or idea without claiming a
  linguistic derivation.
- **Brand-assigned meaning**: the story is intentionally created by the brand.
- **Unverified hypothesis**: promising, but not yet fit for a factual rationale.

Never upgrade one label to a stronger one because the story sounds convincing.
