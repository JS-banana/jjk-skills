# Evaluation

Evaluate against the Brief and the Naming Subject. Do not use one universal
scorecard for every kind of name.

## Hard Gates

Reject or park a candidate when it has:

- A clear mismatch with the subject, audience, or desired feeling
- A likely first reading or spelling failure that matters in the target context
- A rationale that depends on false or unverified provenance
- A serious negative, comic, offensive, or misleading association
- An obvious same-category identity collision
- A restrictive meaning that conflicts with the intended lifespan
- Category imitation so strong that the name cannot form its own identity
- A format failure: invalid package syntax, awkward CLI token, unusable handle,
  or other subject-specific blocker

`Unknown` is not a pass. Park it for research.

## Subject Overlays

- **Company or master brand**: distinction, protectability, category distance,
  cross-market use, and architecture matter most.
- **Product, app, or service**: audience comprehension, emotional fit,
  memorability, and future scope matter most.
- **Project, repository, package, library, or CLI**: typeability, searchability,
  registry collision, shell readability, and technical expectation matter most.
- **Website or domain-led identity**: hearing-to-spelling resilience, URL shape,
  search confusion, and acquisition reality matter most.
- **Community, publication, event, or course**: voice, invitation, recall,
  recurring-series fit, and social use matter most.
- **Feature or member of a family**: parent-brand fit and role clarity matter
  more than standalone grandeur.

## Comparison Dimensions

Select and rank only those relevant to the Brief:

- Strategic fit
- Distinctiveness
- Comprehension
- Pronunciation and hearing-to-spelling resilience
- Memorability
- Emotional or cultural fit
- Visual and written form
- Searchability
- Extensibility
- Naming-system fit
- Preliminary protectability

Use hard gates first. Then compare candidates pairwise against the ranked top
three criteria. Use numbers only when they clarify an explicit tradeoff; a
decimal score does not make taste objective.

## Candidate Card

For each Shortlist candidate, record:

- Name and realistic written forms
- Intended pronunciation and likely alternative readings
- Territory and construction type
- Provenance label with evidence
- One-line rationale
- Gains against the Brief
- Sacrifices or risks
- Preflight status and timestamp when researched

## Recommendation

Recommend one lead and at most two alternatives with different strategic
postures. Include a compact reject log so discarded directions do not silently
return in the next round.
