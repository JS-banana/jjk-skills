# Easy-Vibe Method

This reference turns the Easy-Vibe product-discovery material into demand-radar
operating rules. Use it when a topic is broad, a source only gives ideas, or a
candidate sounds interesting but not yet grounded.

## First Principle

Demand Radar exists to move demand discovery from luck to a repeatable first
diamond: discover a broad problem space, then define a small evidence-backed
job worth considering. It does not design the product, build the MVP, or accept
an idea because it sounds clever.

The accepted shape is:

```text
某类人，在某个场景里，被某一步流程卡住，现在主要靠某种笨办法硬撑。
```

If a candidate cannot be rewritten into that sentence from original evidence,
park or reject it.

## Double Diamond Boundary

Demand Radar covers:

- Discover: collect original user/workflow evidence from varied sources.
- Define: converge to a clear job, pain, workaround, strength, and universality.

Demand Radar does not cover:

- Develop: brainstorming full solution sets.
- Deliver: building or validating an MVP.

Do not let an attractive solution rewrite the problem. If the evidence changes
the problem, update the demand record; if only the solution is exciting, reject.

## JTBD Card

Every kept signal must expose these five elements:

| Element | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Scene | When and where does the user need help? |
| Trigger | What happened that made them seek a solution now? |
| Progress | What state change do they want? |
| Current workaround | What do they use now: product, spreadsheet, manual service, ignoring it, another person? |
| Success standard | What result would make the job feel solved? |

Write the final job as:

```text
当___的时候，我想要___，以便于___。现在我只能通过___勉强完成。
```

## Mom-Test Evidence

Prefer past behavior and current cost over future opinions.

Strong evidence:

- "I paid / hired / subscribed / switched / bought / outsourced."
- "I spent X hours / lost money / missed a deadline / made a risky mistake."
- "I currently use Excel, screenshots, copying, manual service, or a person."
- "I tried A and B, but stopped because..."

Weak or rejectable evidence:

- "Would you use this?" style future intention.
- Polite praise: "sounds useful", "nice idea".
- Generic wish without scene: "I need a better app".
- AI-generated idea with no user or workflow source.

## Source Roles

Idea lists, trends, VC requests, and product directories are leads, not proof.
Use them to decide where to look next. Accepted records require original user,
review, complaint, paid service, procurement, or workflow evidence.

Money/workflow sources are especially valuable:

- Service marketplaces: people paying for repetitive manual work.
- Procurement and tender pages: budgets and requirements already exist.
- Review sites: paid products still leave gaps.
- Industry forums: practitioners describe tedious, risky, or high-cost steps.

## C-Side vs B-Side Reading

C-side signals often hide in feeling: anxiety, fear, confidence, belonging,
status, taste, convenience, or being understood. Keep them only when the feeling
is tied to repeated behavior, workaround, spending, or community reaction.

B-side signals usually show up as time, cost, risk, compliance, coordination, or
manual handoff. Prefer records that name the workflow step and who owns it.

## Anti-Patterns

- Big labels such as `AI for education`, `AI for healthcare`, or `Agent for
  business` without person, scene, job, and workaround.
- Product-first notes: "make an Obsidian alternative" without a job gap.
- Bug-first notes: "plugin cannot load" when it is only one product failing.
- Trend-first notes: "this keyword is rising" without who is paying attention
  and why.
