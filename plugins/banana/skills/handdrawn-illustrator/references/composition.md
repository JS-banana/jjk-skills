# Composition

## Thesis lock

Before composing, write:

```text
This image must communicate: <one complete sentence>.
```

If two independent claims remain, split them into two images.

## Cognitive anchors for articles

Illustrate judgment, turning point, tension, causal mechanism, before/after state, loop, bottleneck or memorable metaphor. Do not illustrate introductions, transitions, repeated examples or paragraphs that are already concrete.

Use 3–5 entries by default:

```yaml
- placement: "after <section or sentence>"
  thesis: "one sentence"
  register: "editorial-scene | explainer-sketch"
  composition: "viewpoint, connected scene, focal action, scale"
  character_role: "none | exact load-bearing action"
  canvas: "paper | charcoal"
  labels: ["short", "exact"]
  aspect_ratio: "16:9"
```

## Register 1: editorial scene

Use by default for judgments, emotions, states and metaphors. Capture one moment before, during or after an action. Establish a ground plane or spatial frame, then place the focal subject and only the props needed to make the thesis legible.

Strong moves include carrying an impossible weight, opening a door too large for the traveler, repairing a broken bridge, following a line through a cavern, or steering an oversized mechanism. Re-invent the physical action for the current thesis; do not treat this list as reusable compositions.

## Register 2: explainer sketch

Use when the relationship itself is the thesis. Limit the scene to 3–5 physical objects or stages. Express connection through paths, pipes, ropes, tracks, doors, balance or shared terrain rather than formal boxes and arrows.

If exact topology, editable geometry, many nodes or numerical precision is required, stop and recommend a diagram tool.

## Anti-collage test

Ask:

1. Do the objects share a believable viewpoint and scale?
2. Does one action or spatial relationship connect them?
3. Could any element be removed without weakening the thesis?
4. Does the eye move through the image without reading a legend?

Remove or redesign until all four answers are satisfactory.
