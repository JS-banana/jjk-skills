# Visual Language

## Identity

Create editorial pencil drawings: quiet, spatial, slightly strange, and composed as one world. The target is not a Notion doodle, a classroom infographic, or a collection of icons.

## Canvas and line

- Default to paper white or very light cool gray with no visible paper photograph.
- Use charcoal black only as an inverse canvas for high-contrast themes; draw in warm white graphite lines.
- Use fine graphite contours with small variations in pressure and slight human wobble.
- Add crosshatching only where it establishes mass, depth or a focal shadow. Leave quiet areas unworked.
- Keep at least roughly one third of the canvas visually quiet.

## Composition

- Prefer asymmetry, foreground/background depth and one obvious focal action.
- Use scale tension: a small figure against an oversized object, structure or landscape when the thesis benefits.
- Give every object the same viewpoint, ground, light and line language.
- Let whitespace carry pace. Do not fill empty areas with decorative marks.

## Palette

- Graphite / charcoal for structure.
- Paper white / warm white for the canvas.
- One warm accent only: pencil yellow, ochre or muted orange.
- Use the accent on one meaningful path, object or state. Never display color names or hex values as text.

## Handwritten text

- Prefer no text when the image already explains itself.
- Otherwise use 0–4 short labels, or one focal phrase.
- Place text inside quiet space and integrate it with the visual balance.
- Keep letterforms casual and restrained, not brush calligraphy, bubbly lettering or a perfect digital UI font.

## Hard bans

- No card grid, sticker collage, floating icon cloud or scrapbook layout.
- No PPT title bar, formal flowchart, dashboard, fake UI or dense legend.
- No glossy 3D, gradient mesh, neon glow, vector stock art or photoreal texture.
- No generic robots, glowing brains, rockets, puzzle pieces or lightbulb metaphors unless the user's subject literally requires them.
- No copied character, watermark, scene, composition or distinctive prop from a reference image.
