# Character — The Little Traveler

Use the traveler as a recurring editorial actor, not a mascot or signature sticker.

## Visual invariants

- A small three-head-tall traveler with a broken halo of soft wool around the head.
- Two short sideways lamb ears; no horns.
- Tiny oval graphite eyes, a small straight mouth and a calm, curious expression.
- A short practical jacket with one asymmetric pocket, a narrow scarf, cuffed trousers and simple ankle boots.
- Graphite and warm white clothing with one ochre scarf or pocket accent.
- A compact silhouette readable at small article size.

Keep the face, proportions, wool silhouette, ear angle, jacket pocket and warm accent stable. Simplify details before changing these anchors.

## Temperament

Quietly serious, observant and willing to do physical work. Friendly without sparkling eyes, exaggerated cuteness or poster poses. Expressions stay small: curiosity, concentration, mild surprise, effort, relief and doubt.

## Load-bearing test

The traveler must push, pull, carry, repair, measure, steer, cross, hold, sort or otherwise perform the conceptual action. If removing the traveler leaves the metaphor fully intact, either give it the decisive action or remove it.

## Interaction rules

- Keep hands visibly connected to tools and feet grounded when contact matters.
- Use at most one hand-held prop unless the thesis requires two.
- Preserve body scale across a set, even when the environment scale changes.
- Do not copy the supplied watermarked character references. Use `assets/character-sheet.png` as the only runtime identity source.
